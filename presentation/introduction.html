Introduction to LHON

Leber Hereditary Optic Neuropathy (LHON) is the most common mitochondrial disorder:

  • Characterized by acute/subacute bilateral vision loss
  • Primarily affects young adults (20-30 years)
  • Caused by pathogenic variants in mitochondrial DNA
  • Three primary variants account for >95% of cases:
    • m.11778G>A in MT-ND4
    • m.3460G>A in MT-ND1
    • m.14484T>C in MT-ND6
  • All affect Complex I of the mitochondrial respiratory chain
  • Incomplete penetrance and variable expressivity
