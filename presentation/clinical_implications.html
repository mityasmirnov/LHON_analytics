Clinical Implications

Transforming genetic counseling:

  • Shift from deterministic to probabilistic risk communication
  • Personalized risk assessment based on individual profiles
  • Emphasis on modifiable risk factors (smoking, alcohol)
  • Reassurance for the vast majority of carriers

Example risk profiles:

  • Low risk: Female, m.14484T>C, non-smoker, light alcohol, haplogroup H → 0.02% risk (1 in 5,000)
  • High risk: Male, m.3460G>A, heavy smoker, heavy alcohol, haplogroup K → 78.3% risk (1 in 1.3)
